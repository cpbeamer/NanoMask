Architecture

Fits where regulated traffic already flows

NanoMask sits between the app and the model/API vendor. Privacy policy, audit, and optional AI controls stay centralized without asking every application team to rewrite their client behavior.

Quick Start

Switch an OpenAI client in minutes

Use the official SDK, but aim it at NanoMask instead of the vendor endpoint.

pip install ./sdk/python from nanomask import OpenAI client = OpenAI(api_key="replace-me", entities=["Jane Doe"]) response = client.responses.create(model="gpt-4o-mini", input="Summarize Jane Doe's chart.")

Node teams get the same shape through `@nanomask/openai`, and CI can call `verify()` before running integration tests.

Positioning

NanoMask vs Presidio vs Private AI

Product Best fit Tradeoff
NanoMask Teams that want a self-hosted reverse proxy, low operational weight, and strong control over outbound AI traffic. Buyer must already be comfortable with proxy insertion instead of in-app libraries everywhere.
Presidio Python-heavy teams building custom detection pipelines into their own services. More assembly required when buyers want a drop-in network boundary rather than a library toolkit.
Private AI Buyers looking for turnkey detection products and broader managed packaging. Heavier vendor-shaped product surface than teams that only want a fast self-hosted privacy firewall.
